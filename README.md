# About this database

In Italy, food supplements are subject to a mandatory notification procedure with the Italian Ministry of Health (Ministero della Salute) under Article 10 of Legislative Decree No. 169/2004.

Once a product has been notified through the Ministry’s electronic notification system, it receives a unique alphanumeric code and is included in the official National Register of Food Supplements (Registro Nazionale degli Integratori Alimentari), which is updated monthly.

This tool provides a fast and searchable interface to the official register, covering more than 100,000 notified food supplements. Users can search by product name, brand, company, or notification code to verify whether a corresponding notification record exists in the Ministry’s database.

This tool is intended to improve transparency by allowing consumers, retailers, healthcare professionals, journalists, and regulators to quickly verify whether a notification record can be found in the official database of the Italian Ministry of Health.

## Why Registration Matters

If a food supplement is marketed in Italy without having undergone the required notification procedure, the responsible operator may be subject to regulatory enforcement actions by the competent authorities. These may include investigations, requests for corrective actions, product withdrawals, administrative sanctions, or other measures provided under applicable Italian and European legislation. 

Because the National Register is the official public record of notified supplements, the absence of a product from the register may indicate that no notification record exists. However, users should be aware that the register is updated periodically and that the legal status of any specific product can ultimately only be determined by the competent authorities.
